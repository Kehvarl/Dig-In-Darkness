# Dig In Darkness
## Narative - Not to be included in published game

While [camping, hiking, exploring] in [region], you stumbled across something wonderful.
The first clue was a standing stone: pale as sand, hard as granite.  A massive shard of rock bearing scars of lightning strikes, and the ravages of untold years of wind and water. The remnants of once deeply-carved motifs now just discolorations marring the honey color of the rocky pillar.

Something about the carvings reminded you of old stories. No real archaeologist pays much heed to the ramblings of treasure hunters, but something about them always called to you. Now you were face to face with possibly the greatest mystery ever to be lost from the pages of history.

You made basecamp in the shadow of the monument, hoping its shattered facade wasn't an omen, and set about exploring the area.  Before long you found... something. A cave entrance leading to a perfectly rectangular space; choked with debris, but a little excavation soon began to turn up relics and clues to far more yet to discover.

Entry:
The entryway is littered with trinkets. Some may be votive offerings, some truly lost treasures, the quantity of them is only surpassed by their almost banal nature. Somewhere in here however are clues that will lead you deeper.

Gallery:
Once you've found the fragmentary writings, decyphered the clues, and unlocked the first of the great seals, you find yourself in a vast columnaded chamber. The walls are covered with fragmentary inscribed tablets.  Some columns hold artwork either breathtaking or subtly terrifying. You could exlore for lifetimes and never learn all the secrets here, and how much more was there when it was new and the carvings unsullied by time? If you can tear yourself away from the tales of the ancients, perhaps you can find the portal deeper

Crypt:
Opening off the Gallery are the Crypts. Burial vaults empty of any inhabitant.  With niches for grave goods, blank plaques, and pristine sarcophagi that have never held a body. Why would a place like this need so many tombs? And whay are they so barren? The Crypts are dead ends, but in them you should find new clues for the Gallery

Chamber:
The Ritual Chamber opens off the gallery. If your lanters grows too dim in here, you may learn something profound.  The walls are the same honey stone as the great marker outside; stone you find nowhere else. The ceiling seems smooth and unmarred, almost polished unless seen in the darkness. What ancient minds saw stars in that formation?

Gates:
The Gates of Sleep open off the Ritual Chamber. A series of 4 great barricades; opened so easily you wonder why anyone would bother with them. Each one covered in faded murals which are certainly worth your time.
